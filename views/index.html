TCM 01:Service Delivery and Continuity of Care

Percent of authorizations having at least one 31 day gap between services

Rationale

To measure the continuity of service provided to CBH-funded TCM and ACT members. Continuity of care is an important measure as we believe that the likelihood of recovery is improved when services are consistent and continuous.

Definition

Percentage of CBH members with one or more 31-day gaps in service observed among all members receiving services from the reporting provider in the reporting period.

Eligible Population

Philadelphia HealthChoices members who had a TCM authorization with multiple paid claims spanning at least 31 days during the reporting period (7/1/2012 – 6/30/2013).

Count the event when...

…at any time during the reporting period (7/1/2012 – 6/30/2013) after the authorization date, the member has had a period of 31 days since the last paid CBH claim on the authorization, unless that claim is the last claim billed to that authorization in the reporting period.

If multiple providers/care levels

If a client is authorized for TCM services with multiple providers or across multiple levels of care during the same time period, the member is considered to have multiple episodes with overlapping time periods. Therefore, a member may be included in the denominator of more than one provider

If there are multiple gaps

A member is only counted in the numerator once, regardless of the number of 31-day gaps in service.

Denominator

Members Served: The total number of members with multiple CBH paid claims for a specific authorization with the given provider and level of care in the reporting period. These paid claims must span at least 31 days in the reporting period.

Numerator

Members with Observed Gaps in Service: The number of members for whom one or more 31-day gaps in service are observed for the reporting provider and given level of care.
